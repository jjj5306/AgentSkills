# Writing Review Checklist

Use this checklist only when a document is long, important, or difficult to assess from the core workflow alone.

## Reader And Purpose

- Who is expected to read this?
- What decision, action, or understanding should the reader leave with?
- Is the current level of detail appropriate for that reader?
- Does the document need to inform, decide, persuade, record, or instruct?

## Consistency

- Do scope statements match later examples and conclusions?
- Are the same terms used for the same concept?
- Are constraints, assumptions, and exceptions stable throughout?
- Does the conclusion follow from the evidence provided?

## Readability

- Can the reader scan headings and understand the document shape?
- Are long paragraphs hiding multiple topics?
- Are lists parallel and grouped by the same principle?
- Are key points placed before details where appropriate?

## Concision

- Are multiple sections repeating the same background?
- Can caveats or context be reduced without losing meaning?
- Are examples necessary, or are they compensating for unclear prose?
- Does any section explain process when the reader needs outcome?

## Accuracy And Verification

- Which claims depend on external facts, dates, numbers, policies, APIs, or current product behavior?
- Which claims are stated more strongly than the evidence supports?
- Are assumptions clearly marked as assumptions?
- Are stale references, outdated names, or version-sensitive statements present?

## Clarity

- Are actors, responsibilities, and ownership clear?
- Are "this", "that", "it", "the issue", or "the above" ambiguous?
- Are conditions and boundaries explicit?
- Are requested actions stated directly?

## Structure And Emphasis

- Is the main conclusion visible early enough?
- Are risks, decisions, blockers, and asks easy to find?
- Should related sections be merged or split?
- Is any important point buried in a low-visibility paragraph?

## Skills And Workflow Instructions

- Does the description make the trigger conditions clear before the body is loaded?
- Are scope boundaries, stop rules, and user-confirmation rules explicit?
- Are validation commands executable in the expected environment?
- Are prerequisites, paths, tools, and environment variables concrete enough to reuse?
- Could duplicated or conflicting instructions confuse a future agent?
- Is the output contract clear enough to judge whether the task is done?

## Output Discipline

- Report only findings that materially improve the document.
- Group minor issues instead of listing every occurrence.
- Do not provide rewrite examples unless explicitly requested.
- Use "확인 필요" for uncertain factual claims instead of guessing.
